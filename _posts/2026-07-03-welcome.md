Welcome, and thanks for stopping by.

I'm a robotics researcher at Indiana University working on field robotics and
autonomous racing. This is where I'll write in a looser register than a paper
allows — short notes on problems I'm chewing on, things I've learned building
autonomy stacks, and the occasional detour into whatever else has my attention.

A few topics I expect to come back to:

- **Navigation on rough terrain** — what actually breaks when a robot leaves the
  sidewalk, and why maps aren't always the answer.
- **Autonomous racing** — control, estimation, and the very different problem of
  being right *fast enough* at 200+ km/h.
- **Research life** — reading, tooling, and lessons that don't fit in a
  related-work section.

If any of that sounds interesting, check back now and then — or find my papers in
the [Publications](/#publications) section and my photos on
[Photography](/photography/).
