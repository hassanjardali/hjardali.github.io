We present a method for autonomous navigation on uneven terrain using a sparse Gaussian Process (SGP) local perception model. Trained online on local point-cloud observations, the model learns the terrain elevation profile and extracts feasible navigation subgoals around the robot. A safety-aware cost function keeps the robot's roll and pitch angles bounded while guiding it toward its goal, and the approach runs in real time in both simulation and real-world experiments.
