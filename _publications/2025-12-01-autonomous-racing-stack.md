This paper introduces the Autonomous Race Stack (ARS), developed by the IU Luddy Autonomous Racing team for the Indy Autonomous Challenge (IAC). We describe the modular architecture and its evolution across three iterations (ARS1–ARS3), provide a detailed performance evaluation of control, perception, and estimation across oval and road-course environments, and release a high-speed, multi-sensor dataset. The stack was validated on real speedways at speeds up to 260 km/h.
